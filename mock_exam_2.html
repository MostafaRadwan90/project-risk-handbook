Section A: Short Answer Questions
Question 1 (5 Marks)
What are the main purposes of conducting a Risk Audit during the Control Risk process?

Model Answer:

The main purposes of conducting a Risk Audit during the Control Risk process are to:

  • Examine and document the effectiveness of risk responses: To determine if the implemented responses are working as intended in dealing with identified risks and their root causes.
  • Evaluate the effectiveness of the overall risk management process: To assess if the defined risk management procedures (as outlined in the Risk Management Plan) are being followed correctly and are adequate.
  • Identify lessons learned: To capture insights from the risk management activities that can be applied to improve risk management on the current project or future projects.
  • Ensure compliance: To verify that risk management policies and procedures are being adhered to by the project team and risk owners.
  • Validate risk assessments: To check the accuracy and continued relevance of prior risk assessments and risk ratings.

Rationale: Based on Handbook Chapter 9, Slide 70.

Question 2 (5 Marks)
Briefly describe three tools or techniques used for Risk Identification, other than brainstorming.

Model Answer:

Three tools or techniques used for Risk Identification, other than brainstorming, are:

  1. Delphi Technique: An information gathering technique where a panel of experts participate anonymously through multiple rounds of questionnaires. Responses are summarized and re-circulated to achieve a consensus on key project risks without the influence of dominant personalities.
  2. SWOT Analysis: Examines the project from the perspectives of internal Strengths and Weaknesses, and external Opportunities and Threats. This strategic analysis helps identify a broad range of risks, including both positive and negative ones.
  3. Checklist Analysis: Uses a pre-developed list of potential risk categories or specific risks based on historical information, lessons learned from similar projects, or industry best practices. The lowest level of a Risk Breakdown Structure (RBS) can often serve as a checklist.

Rationale: Based on Handbook Chapter 4, Slides 26, 27, & 28.

Question 3 (5 Marks)
Explain the difference between a "Contingency Plan" and a "Fallback Plan" in risk response planning.

Model Answer:

  • Contingency Plan: This is a planned response ("Plan B") that is developed in advance to be executed *if* a specific identified risk event occurs (i.e., a risk trigger is activated). It outlines the specific actions to take to deal with the impact of that particular risk.
  • Fallback Plan: This is a plan developed as a backup to a primary risk response or for an accepted risk. It is implemented *if* the primary risk response strategy proves to be ineffective in managing the risk, or if an accepted risk occurs and its impact is more severe than anticipated or can no longer be passively accepted. It's essentially a plan for when "Plan A" (the primary response) or simple acceptance fails.

In essence, a contingency plan addresses a specific risk materializing as expected, while a fallback plan addresses the failure or inadequacy of a planned response or the unexpected severity of an accepted risk.

Rationale: Based on Handbook Chapter 7, Slide 52.

Section B: Multiple Choice Questions
4. Which of the following is a key characteristic of the "Exploit" strategy for positive risks?
  • A. Allocating ownership of the opportunity to a third party.
  • B. Increasing the probability or positive impact of the opportunity.
  • C. Ensuring the opportunity definitely happens.
  • D. Acknowledging the opportunity but not actively pursuing it.

Correct Answer: C

Rationale: The Exploit strategy aims to eliminate uncertainty and make the realization of the opportunity certain. (Handbook Chapter 8, Slide 64)

5. The primary output of the Identify Risks process is the:
  • A. Risk Management Plan
  • B. Probability and Impact Matrix
  • C. Risk Register
  • D. Risk Response Plan

Correct Answer: C

Rationale: The Risk Register is where identified risks and their characteristics are initially documented. (Handbook Chapter 4, Slide 29)

6. A quantified loss potential of a project, often calculated as Probability x Impact, is known as:
  • A. Risk Tolerance
  • B. Risk Appetite
  • C. Risk Exposure
  • D. Risk Breakdown Structure

Correct Answer: C

Rationale: Risk Exposure is the quantified potential loss (or gain for opportunities), often calculated as EMV. (Handbook Chapter 1, Slide 9)

7. In a Safety Management Plan (SMP), procedures for how accidents and near misses will be investigated to prevent recurrence fall under:
  • A. Safety Policy and Objectives
  • B. Emergency Response
  • C. Accident Investigation Procedures
  • D. Safety Inspections and Audits

Correct Answer: C

Rationale: This directly describes the purpose of Accident Investigation Procedures within an SMP. (Handbook Chapter 11, Slide 93)

8. A Tornado Diagram is a common way to display the results of:
  • A. Qualitative Risk Analysis
  • B. Monte Carlo Simulation
  • C. Sensitivity Analysis
  • D. Risk Identification

Correct Answer: C

Rationale: Tornado diagrams visually represent the sensitivity of project objectives to different risks, which is a key output of Sensitivity Analysis. (Handbook Chapter 6, Slide 50)

9. Which type of safety measure tracks safety performance *after* an incident has already happened?
  • A. Leading Indicator
  • B. Proactive Indicator
  • C. Predictive Indicator
  • D. Lagging Indicator

Correct Answer: D

Rationale: Lagging indicators are reactive and measure outcomes or failures after an event. (Handbook Chapter 13, Slide 111)

10. During a project, an unforeseen technical issue arises that was not in the risk register. The project manager quickly develops and implements a solution. This solution is best described as a:
  • A. Contingency plan
  • B. Fallback plan
  • C. Workaround
  • D. Residual risk

Correct Answer: C

Rationale: A workaround is an unplanned response to a threat that has occurred for which no prior response was planned. (Handbook Chapter 7, Slide 53)
