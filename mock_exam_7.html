Section A: Short Answer Questions
Question 1 (5 Marks)
What are "Leading Indicators" and "Lagging Indicators" in safety performance measurement? Provide one example for each.

Model Answer:

Leading Indicators:

  • Definition: These are proactive safety measures that aim to identify and prevent safety hazards *before* incidents occur. They focus on future safety performance and activities that can improve safety outcomes.
  • Example: The number of safety training sessions conducted per month, or the percentage of workers correctly using Personal Protective Equipment (PPE).

Lagging Indicators:

  • Definition: These are reactive safety measures that track safety performance *after* an incident has already happened. They measure failures and past outcomes.
  • Example: The Total Recordable Incident Rate (TRIR) for a given period, or the number of Lost Time Injuries (LTIs).

Rationale: Based on Handbook Chapter 13, Slides 111, 112, & 113.

Question 2 (5 Marks)
Describe the purpose and key elements of a Construction Phase Health and Safety Plan.

Model Answer:

Purpose:

The Construction Phase Health and Safety Plan is typically developed by the main contractor. Its purpose is to detail how health and safety will be managed practically during the construction phase of the project, translating the principles of the overall H&S plan and pre-tender H&S plan into specific actions and arrangements for the site.

Key Elements/Principles for Development:

  • Avoid risks altogether where possible (elimination).
  • Evaluate risks that cannot be avoided (risk assessment).
  • Combat risks at source (e.g., removing a problem rather than just posting a warning sign).
  • Adapt work to the individual, and give them control over speed of work where possible.
  • Make use of technological progress to improve safety.
  • Give priority to solutions protecting the whole workplace rather than individuals (collective protection over personal protection).
  • Ensure everyone knows what they need to do to remain safe and healthy (communication, training, information).
  • Foster a positive organizational safety culture where risk control is integrated into normal work.

Specific contents would include site-specific hazard controls, emergency procedures, roles and responsibilities for H&S on site, and arrangements for monitoring and review.

Rationale: Based on Handbook Chapter 10, Slides 82 & 83.

Question 3 (5 Marks)
What are the main Inputs, Tools & Techniques, and Outputs (ITTOs) for the "Identify Risks" process?

Model Answer:

Inputs to Identify Risks:

  • Project Management Plan (especially Risk Management Plan, Scope Baseline, Cost Management Plan, Schedule Management Plan, etc.)
  • Project Documents (e.g., Assumption Log, Lessons Learned Register, Stakeholder Register, Requirements Documentation)
  • Enterprise Environmental Factors (EEFs) (e.g., industry studies, published checklists)
  • Organizational Process Assets (OPAs) (e.g., lessons learned from past projects, risk checklists)

Tools & Techniques for Identify Risks:

  • Expert Judgment
  • Data Gathering (e.g., Brainstorming, Checklists, Interviews, Delphi Technique)
  • Data Analysis (e.g., Root Cause Analysis, SWOT Analysis, Assumption and Constraint Analysis)
  • Diagramming Techniques (e.g., Cause and Effect Diagrams, Flowcharts, Influence Diagrams)
  • Meetings (Risk Workshops)

Outputs of Identify Risks:

  • Risk Register (Initial): Containing a list of identified risks, potential risk owners, and potential responses.
  • Risk Report (may be initiated or updated)
  • Project Documents Updates (e.g., Assumption Log, Lessons Learned Register)

Rationale: Based on Handbook Chapter 4, Slide 23 and PMBOK principles.

8. Which of the following is FALSE regarding risk management?
  • 1. All identified risks are maintained in the risk register.
  • 2. Risk prioritization occurs in Qualitative Risk Analysis.
  • 3. Risk prioritization occurs in Quantitative Risk Analysis.
  • 4. Low priority risks are maintained in a watch list.

Correct Answer: 3

Rationale: 1. True, the risk register is the central repository. 2. True, qualitative analysis prioritizes risks. 4. True, watchlists are for low-priority risks. 3. False, while quantitative analysis provides numerical data that can further refine prioritization or identify key drivers of overall risk, the primary *act* of prioritization based on probability and impact scales is a hallmark of Qualitative Risk Analysis. Quantitative analysis provides more detailed numerical insights on already prioritized risks. (Handbook Chapter 5 & 6)

9. A project manager has assembled the project team, identified 56 risks on the project, determined what would trigger the risks, rated them on a risk rating matrix, tested their assumptions and assessed the quality of the data used. The team is continuing to move through the risk management process. What has the project manager forgotten to do before moving to detailed response planning?
  • 1. Simulation
  • 2. Risk mitigation (This is a response strategy, not a forgotten step in analysis)
  • 3. Overall risk ranking for the project (Prioritized list)
  • 4. Involving of other stakeholders (This should happen throughout, but a specific output is missing)

Correct Answer: 3

Rationale: After rating risks on a matrix (Qualitative Analysis), a key output is the overall risk ranking or prioritized list of risks. This step is crucial before deciding on specific mitigation or other responses, or moving to quantitative analysis like simulation if needed. (Handbook Chapter 5, Section 5.4)

10. Risk monitoring and control involves all of the following tasks except:
  • 1. Identifying new risks.
  • 2. Ensuring the execution of risk plans.
  • 3. Contingency planning.
  • 4. Evaluating and execution of risk plans.

Correct Answer: 3

Rationale: Contingency *planning* (developing the plans) is done in the "Plan Risk Responses" process. Risk Monitoring and Control involves *implementing* or *executing* those plans if risk triggers occur, identifying new risks, and evaluating the effectiveness of risk plans. (Handbook Chapter 9, Slide 68)
