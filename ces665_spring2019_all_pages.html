1. Risks will be identified during which risk management process(es)?
  • A. Perform Quantitative Risk Analysis and Identify Risks
  • B. Identify Risks and Control Risks
  • C. Perform Qualitative Risk Analysis and Control Risks
  • D. Identify Risks

Correct Answer: D

Rationale: 'Identify Risks' is the primary process for discovering and documenting risks.

(Page 1/4)
2. Risk tolerances are determined to help:
  • A. The team ranks the project risks
  • B. The project manager estimates the project.
  • C. The team schedules the project.
  • D. Management know how other managers will act on the project.

Correct Answer: A

Rationale: Risk tolerances define acceptable variation, helping rank risks based on whether their impact exceeds these tolerances.

(Page 1/4)
3. Purchasing insurance is considered an example of a risk
  • A. Mitigation.
  • B. Acceptance.
  • C. Transfer.
  • D. Avoidance.

Correct Answer: C

Rationale: Insurance shifts financial impact to a third party (insurer), a form of risk transfer. (Handbook Chapter 7, Slide 60)

(Page 1/4)
4. Workarounds are determined during which risk management process?
  • A. Identify Risks
  • B. Plan Risk Responses
  • C. Perform Quantitative Risk Analysis
  • D. Control Risks

Correct Answer: D

Rationale: Workarounds are unplanned responses implemented during Control Risks when unforeseen risks occur or planned responses fail. (Handbook Chapter 7, Slide 53; Chapter 9, Slide 73)

(Page 1/4)
5. A project manager has assembled the project team, identified 56 risks on the project, determined what would trigger the risks, rated them on a risk rating matrix, tested their assumptions and assessed the quality of the data used. The team is continuing to move through the risk management process. What has the project manager forgotten to do?
  • A. Simulation
  • B. Overall risk ranking for the project
  • C. Risk mitigation
  • D. Involving of other stakeholders

Correct Answer: B

Rationale: After rating risks (Qualitative Analysis), establishing an overall risk ranking/prioritized list is a key output before detailed response planning or further quantitative analysis.

(Page 1/4)
6. Deflection or transfer of a risk to another party is part of which of the following risk response categories?
  • A. Mitigation
  • B. Avoidance
  • C. Acceptance
  • D. Analysis

Correct Answer: C

Rationale: Problematic question if 'Transfer' isn't an option. 'Deflection' implies shifting. If no formal transfer, the original party might passively accept residual consequences. (Best fit from limited options).

(Page 1/4)
7. You are in the build phase of the project. But it has run into several unanticipated problems. Several risks have surfaced which you had not anticipated earlier. The project is over-budget and behind schedule. What should you do?
  • A. Create updates to risk response plan
  • B. Create a revised project plan
  • C. Perform risk response audits
  • D. Perform an updated risk identification and analysis

Correct Answer: D

Rationale: New, unanticipated risks require a fresh cycle of identification and analysis before plans can be appropriately updated.

(Page 1/4)
8. While doing risk response planning, you realize that there is a very critical risk which may have a high impact on the project completion. So, you create a fallback plan which could include any of the following conditions EXCEPT:
  • A. Subcontracting the project to an outside Vendor
  • B. Developing Alternative Options
  • C. Allocation of Contingency Reserves
  • D. Changing project scope

Correct Answer: A

Rationale: Subcontracting the entire project is a major strategic shift or primary transfer, not typically a mere condition within a fallback plan for a specific risk's failed response. Other options are more aligned with fallback actions.

9. Risks because of implementing a risk response are called:
  • A. Unidentifiable Risks
  • B. Secondary Risks
  • C. Residual Risks
  • D. Accepted Risks

Correct Answer: B

Rationale: Secondary risks are new risks arising directly from implementing a risk response. (Handbook Chapter 7, Slide 52)

10. When analyzing risks using the certainty vs uncertainty spectrum, the term "unknown-unknown is best described as:
  • A. At the extreme end of the uncertainty spectrum. They will affect you, although you have no control over them.
  • B. Items or situations whose existence cannot imagine ("who knew?")
  • C. Items that will affect you, although you are not able to predict how or how much they will affect you?
  • D. Rework

Correct Answer: B

Rationale: 'Unknown-unknowns' are unforeseeable risks. 'Who knew?' captures this. (Handbook Chapter 7, Slide 53 - Management Reserve for unknown-unknowns)

(Page 2/4)
11. Your risk analysis involving your project scope reflects your project has an 70% chance of completing the scope by a specified date with an 80% chance of receiving approval of the scope by a certain date. Based on this data, what is the probability of both events occurring?
  • A. 0.75
  • B. 0.8
  • C. 0.5
  • D. 0.56

Correct Answer: D

Rationale: For independent events, P(A and B) = P(A) * P(B). So, 0.70 * 0.80 = 0.56.

(Page 2/4)
12. Which is not a tool or technique used in the quantitative risk analysis process?
  • A. Interviewing.
  • B. Sensitivity analysis
  • C. SWOT analysis.
  • D. Simulation.

Correct Answer: C

Rationale: SWOT is for risk identification. Quantitative analysis uses techniques like interviewing (for data), sensitivity analysis, and simulation. (Handbook Chapter 4, Slide 27; Chapter 6, Slides 47, 49)

(Page 2/4)
13. A risk was identified that "Rebecca 'is the only person in the company that has the expertise to complete a critical task on your project. Rebecca advised during the planning phase that she is scheduled for elective surgery at an unknown date. If the hospital calls, she will go on medical leave for 3 months. Of course, the hospital calls and Rebecca go on medical leave. Since the task is on the critical path you need to acquire a consultant as soon as possible. To do this, the project manager
  • A. Follow the legal requirements set up by the company for using outside services.
  • B. Bypass the company procedure since the task is on the critical path and you don't have time to go throw the normal process.
  • C. Time is of the essence. Expedite and go directly to your preferred consultant.
  • D. Ask his or her manager what to do

Correct Answer: A

Rationale: Professional conduct requires following company policies and legal requirements, even in urgent situations, to avoid further complications.

(Page 2/4)
14. A contingency plan
  • A. Identifies alternative strategies to be used to ensure project success if a specified risk event occurs.
  • B. Is a work around.
  • C. Is part of the management reserve
  • D. Is the same as a fallback plan.

Correct Answer: A

Rationale: A contingency plan is a pre-defined set of actions for a specific risk event. (Handbook Chapter 7, Slide 52)

(Page 2/4)
15. Risk monitoring and control involves all the following tasks except:
  • A. Identifying new risks.
  • B. Evaluating and execution of risk plans.
  • C. Ensuring the execution of risk plans.
  • D. Contingency planning.

Correct Answer: D

Rationale: Contingency planning (developing the plans) is done in 'Plan Risk Responses'. Monitoring and Control executes these plans if triggered. (Handbook Chapter 9, Slide 68)

(Page 2/4)
16. What is the most effective document to review to learn about the project's procedure for managing risk?
  • A. A risk management plan.
  • B. A risk analysis plan
  • C. A project plans
  • D. A risk assessment study.

Correct Answer: A

Rationale: The Risk Management Plan outlines how risk management activities will be structured and performed. (Handbook Chapter 2, Slide 14)

(Page 2/4)
17. Which document is developed along the risk management processes from identifying risks through performing qualitative risk analysis to control risk?
  • A. List of risk triggers.
  • B. Risk mitigation
  • C. Risk register.
  • D. Decision tree.

Correct Answer: C

Rationale: The Risk Register is a living document updated throughout all risk management processes. (Handbook Chapter 4, Slide 29)

18. As the project manager in a software project, which is currently initiated, you want to assess high-level risks. What should you do?
  • A. Develop the project charter and a risk management plan to start identifying risks based on both and other documents.
  • B. Identify and analyze risk events using qualitative and quantitative techniques.
  • C. Develop contingency plans and fallback plans in case the original plan proves wrong.
  • D. Discuss the risks documented in your risk register with the project key stakeholders.

Correct Answer: A

Rationale: For high-level risk assessment at initiation, establishing the foundational documents (charter for high-level info, RMP for process) is the starting point.

19. You are running a major project with four sub-projects. Each of sub-project manager has developed a risk management plan, identified many risks, analyzed them, and planned for risk response. It is now two months later. What should you not do?
  • A. Ensure that they handle all risks to save you from project level risk control.
  • B. Make sure that the sub-project managers monitor their project work for new and changing risk.
  • C. Make sure that the sub-project managers keep track of the identified risks and those on the watch list.
  • D. Make sure that the sub-project managers monitor triggers conditions for contingency plans.

Correct Answer: A

Rationale: The overall PM retains responsibility for project-level risks and cannot completely delegate all risk control to sub-project managers without oversight.

20. During risk identification sessions, you and your team identify more than 150 risks for your project. You are afraid that evaluating each of these risks quantitatively will cost you an enormous amount of time, while not all of them are relevant and important enough to justify such measure. What should you do next?
  • A. Identify risk triggers where possible. Analyze only those risks quantitatively for which no trigger could be found.
  • B. Use qualitative risk analysis to prioritize risks for further action, such as quantitative risk analysis.
  • C. Assess the probability of each risk qualitatively and further analyze only those risks with high probability.
  • D. Assess the potential impact of each risk qualitatively and further analyze only those risks with high impact.

Correct Answer: B

Rationale: Qualitative analysis is used to prioritize a large list of identified risks to focus further efforts like quantitative analysis on high-priority ones. (Handbook Chapter 5)

21. You identified a technical risk in your project and assigned a contingency for that. Planning contingency reserve is part of which risk response strategy?
  • A. Active risk mitigation.
  • B. Passive risk acceptance.
  • C. Passive risk avoidance.
  • D. Active risk acceptance.

Correct Answer: D

Rationale: Establishing a contingency reserve is a form of active acceptance. (Handbook Chapter 8, Slide 63)

22. Being the project manager in a high-risk electronics project with a lot of new technologies, you developed a risk management plan and identified risk which you documented in the risk register. Then the risks were analyzed, and response was planned. During risk control meetings, it gets obvious that the documents you created are not very helpful. What have you probably done wrong?
  • A. You failed to use RBS.
  • B. You did not identify triggers.
  • C. You did the first processes alone.
  • D. You did not calculate EMVs.

Correct Answer: A

Rationale: An RBS provides structure for risk identification and categorization, making subsequent documentation and analysis more useful. Its absence can lead to disorganized and unhelpful risk documents.

23. During planning processes, you used Monte Carlo simulation to quantitatively assess cost and schedule risks of your project. During risk control, you repeat the technique, and it leads to different results. What should not be the reason for that?
  • A. Some assumptions during planning have meanwhile become fact-based knowledge, so that the risks related to them have vanished or have become certain problems.
  • B. New risks have been identified. This influence the input data used for Monte Carlo simulation in a way which was not predictable at the time when the simulation was run.
  • C. Some constraints have been identified originally, but their influence on the project was unclear when the simulation was run for the first time. By now, the team understands these constraints much better and has been able to adjust the simulation.
  • D. Some dummy activities in the network logic have an element of uncertainty, which get bigger over time. While the project proceeds, it gets even harder to predict how the team members assigned to them will perform.

Correct Answer: D

Rationale: Options A, B, and C describe valid reasons why Monte Carlo inputs would change. Option D describes increasing general project uncertainty but doesn't directly explain differing Monte Carlo results unless this new uncertainty is explicitly re-modeled as input.

24. When is the passive risk acceptance an appropriate approach?
  • A. When the likeliness of risk occurrence is very low.
  • B. When the risk occurrence is hard to identify.
  • C. When it best to deal with a risk as it occurs.
  • D. When the risk cannot be assessed or analyzed.

Correct Answer: A

Rationale: Passive acceptance is suitable for low-priority risks, often those with very low probability or very low impact. (Handbook Chapter 8, Slide 63)

25. Which of the following is true about risks?
  • A. Risks always have negative impact and not positive
  • B. Risk impact should be considered, but probability of occurrence is not important
  • C. The risk register documents all the identified risks in detail
  • D. Risk Response Plan is another name for Risk Management Plan.

Correct Answer: C

26. If a project has a 60% chance of a U.S. $100,000 profit and a 40% chance of a U.S. $100,000 loss, the expected monetary value of the project is?
  • A. $20000 profit
  • B. $100000 profit
  • C. $40000 loss
  • D. $50000 loss

Correct Answer: A

Rationale: EMV = (0.60 * $100,000) + (0.40 * -$100,000) = $60,000 - $40,000 = $20,000 profit. (Handbook Chapter 6)

(Page 4/4)
27. Which of these is accurate regarding risk management?
  • A. The attitudes of individuals and organizations must not be a factor affecting risk management
  • B. Organizations are not likely to perceive risk as a threat to project success
  • C. It is a passive activity in project management
  • D. It has its origins in the uncertainty present in all projects

Correct Answer: D

Rationale: Risk management fundamentally addresses project uncertainty. Attitudes (A) are crucial (risk appetite/tolerance). Organizations do perceive threats (B). It's a proactive activity (C). (Handbook Chapter 1, 2)

28. Which of these is a valid response to negative risks and not positive risks?
  • A. Exploit
  • B. Mitigate
  • C. Enhance
  • D. Share

33. Which of these statements about Risk in a project is correct?
  • A. Risk responses reflect an organization's perceived balance between risk taking and risk avoidance.
