Question 1 (5 Marks)
What are the main Inputs, Tools & Techniques, and Outputs (ITTOs) for the "Control Risks" (or Monitor Risks) process?

Model Answer:

Inputs to Control Risks:

  • Project Management Plan (especially Risk Management Plan)
  • Project Documents (e.g., Risk Register, Risk Report, Issue Log, Lessons Learned Register)
  • Work Performance Data
  • Work Performance Reports

Tools & Techniques for Control Risks:

  • Expert Judgment
  • Data Analysis (e.g., Technical Performance Analysis, Reserve Analysis, Variance and Trend Analysis)
  • Risk Reassessment
  • Risk Audits
  • Meetings (e.g., Risk Review Meetings)

Outputs of Control Risks:

  • Work Performance Information
  • Change Requests (for corrective/preventive actions)
  • Project Management Plan Updates
  • Project Documents Updates (especially Risk Register, Risk Report, Lessons Learned Register, Issue Log)
  • Organizational Process Assets Updates

Rationale: Based on Handbook Chapter 9, Slide 67 and PMBOK principles.

Question 2 (5 Marks)
Explain the "Loss Control" concept in safety management and list its three main aspects as per the course material.

Model Answer:

Loss Control Concept:

Loss Control in safety management refers to any measure or action taken to reduce the frequency, severity, or impact of accidental losses arising from the risks associated with business operations or projects. It is a proactive and reactive set of strategies aimed at preventing losses from occurring and minimizing the consequences of losses that do occur.

Three Main Aspects of Loss Control:

  1. The prevention of loss exposures: This involves identifying potential hazards and implementing measures to eliminate or control them before any loss-producing event can happen (e.g., designing safer processes, providing safety training).
  2. The reduction of loss when loss-producing events occur: This focuses on minimizing the severity or impact of an accident or incident once it has started (e.g., emergency response procedures, fire suppression systems, first aid).
  3. The termination or avoidance of risk: This involves completely eliminating a hazardous activity or condition, or choosing not to engage in it, thereby preventing any possibility of associated loss.

Rationale: Based on Handbook Chapter 11, Slide 90.

Question 3 (5 Marks)
What is the purpose of a "Pre-Use Inspection" in safety management, and what key activities are involved in implementing it?

Model Answer:

Purpose of Pre-Use Inspection:

A Pre-Use Inspection involves checks performed by an operator or user on equipment, machinery, or systems *before* each use. Its purpose is to verify that the equipment is in safe operating condition, that all safety features are functional, and to identify any defects or damage that could lead to an accident or failure during operation. It's a critical proactive step to ensure safety for systems vital to proper operation that could be damaged or become substandard through regular use.

Key Activities in Implementing Pre-Use Inspections:

  • Designate equipment: Clearly identify which pieces of equipment or systems require pre-use checks.
  • Provide forms/checklists: Develop standardized checklists tailored to each type of equipment, guiding the operator through the necessary checks.
  • Train operators: Ensure operators are properly trained on how to perform the pre-use inspections correctly and what to do if defects are found.
  • Audit compliance: Periodically check that pre-use inspections are being consistently and correctly performed and documented.

Rationale: Based on Handbook Chapter 14, Slide 129.

5. As a project manager, you identify two stakeholders for a large construction project: Laura, who has a lot of interest but no significant authority, and Sean, who is disinterested and also has no significant authority. Using a Power/Interest grid, what is the best approach to manage them?
  • 1. Both Sean and Laura should be kept informed.
  • 2. Sean should be monitored while Laura should be kept informed.
  • 3. Both Sean and Laura should be monitored.
  • 4. Sean should be kept informed while Laura should be monitored.

Correct Answer: 2

Rationale: Laura (Low Power, High Interest) should be Kept Informed. Sean (Low Power, Low Interest) should be Monitored with minimal effort. (Handbook Chapter 4, Slide 24)

From provided question bank.
6. You are managing a high-risk electronics project with new technologies. You developed a risk management plan, identified risks in a risk register, analyzed them, and planned responses. During risk control meetings, it becomes obvious your risk documents are not very helpful. What have you probably done wrong?
  • 1. You failed to use RBS (Risk Breakdown Structure).
  • 2. You did not identify triggers.
  • 3. You did the first processes alone.
  • 4. You did not calculate EMVs.

Correct Answer: 1

Rationale: Failing to use an RBS can lead to poorly organized and less comprehensive risk identification and documentation, making the risk register less helpful. While other options could be issues, an unhelpful overall risk documentation structure often points to a lack of systematic categorization like an RBS provides. Calculating EMVs (4) is part of quantitative analysis, not always mandatory. Identifying triggers (2) is important for responses but the core issue is "documents not helpful". Doing processes alone (3) is bad practice but an RBS is a structural tool. (Handbook Chapter 3, Slide 16)

7. Risks are identified during which of the following risk management processes?
  • A. Perform Quantitative Risk Analysis and Identify Risks
  • B. Identify Risks and Control Risks
  • C. Perform Qualitative Risk Analysis and Control Risks
  • D. Identify Risks only

Correct Answer: B

Rationale: "Identify Risks" is the primary process for initial identification. New risks are also identified and existing ones re-evaluated during the "Control Risks" (or Monitor Risks) process. Therefore, B is the most comprehensive correct answer. (Handbook Chapter 4 and Chapter 9, Slide 68)

From provided question bank.
8. Risk tolerances are primarily determined to help:
  • A. The team ranks the project risks.
  • B. The project manager estimates the project cost.
  • C. Determine which risks are less important.
  • D. Determine which risks need a response plan.

Correct Answer: A (and by extension D)

Rationale: Risk tolerances define acceptable deviations. Risks exceeding these tolerances are typically ranked higher (A) and are more likely to require a response plan (D). Option A is a direct outcome of applying tolerance levels in qualitative analysis. (Handbook Chapter 1, Slide 7)

9. Purchasing insurance is considered an example of which risk response category?
  • A. Mitigation
  • B. Acceptance
  • C. Transfer
  • D. Avoidance

Correct Answer: C

Rationale: Purchasing insurance shifts the financial impact of a specific risk to a third party (the insurer), which is the definition of risk transfer. (Handbook Chapter 7, Slide 60)
